About Me!

Hello! My name is Kathryn Le. I am a Tompkins Square Middle School alumni and a current junior in the Bronx High School of Science who is interested in computer science and scientific research.



I participate in the Math and Computer Science Research Track and am currently working with Dr. Hernan Makse of CCNY.

What I'm currently working on...

Research

As part of the Math and Computer Science Research Track offered at my school, I am working with Dr. Hernan Makse of CCNY on a research project that I will eventually submit into the Intel Regeneron Research competition. I hope to combine my knowledge in biophysics and machine learning to conduct meaningful research under the mentorship of Dr. Hernan Makse and Dr. Vladimir Shapovalov.

Blog

This experience inspired me to create my own blog so that I could spread awareness about the current events surrounding technology and AI. I also wanted to emphasize in my articles the potential influence of these rapidly advanicing technologies. See my articles here.

Machine Learning

In the summer of 2020, I attended the Machine Learning course in the Columbia Summer Immersion Program. This amazing experience taught me so much about machine learning, data science and the real-world applications and potential that it has. I created 2 projects (view them here) that I plan to expand in the future. Using the knowledge I gained from this program I hope to create more projects and conduct more research to keep learning and improving. I am also an unofficial board member of the Machine Learning Club and I wish to share the knowledge I learned to club members as well as learn more from those with more experience.

Steel City Codes

The elementary and middle schools that I attended sadly did not offer the opportunity for me to learn computer science. Aspiring to become and computer science major, I felt that I was in a slight disadvantage starting to learn coding in the first year of high school. This is why I decided to start an NYC chapter stemming from Steel City Codes, a non-profit student-led organization that sets out to provide free computer science classes from beginner to intermediate level to students in schools that do not provide this opportunity and to areas that with low-income families and minority groups.
